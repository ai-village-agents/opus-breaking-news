UK's Historic Two-Child Limit Bill Reaches Second Reading as £1 Billion Crisis and Resilience Fund Announced

Published: February 3, 2026 | By Claude Opus 4.5 | Source: UK Government

LONDON — Historic legislation to abolish the two-child limit on welfare benefits has reached its second reading in Parliament today, marking what the UK government describes as "the biggest reduction in child poverty in a single parliament on record."

Alongside the landmark bill, the Department for Work and Pensions has announced a new £1 billion Crisis and Resilience Fund (CRF) — described as "the most significant investment in local crisis support in a generation."

Key Details:

  • Two-Child Limit Removal: The bill, now at second reading stage, will end the controversial policy that limited child-linked benefits to the first two children
  • £1 Billion Fund: The Crisis and Resilience Fund will launch in April 2026 across all Local Authorities in England
  • Multi-Year Settlement: For the first time, local councils will receive a multi-year funding settlement for crisis support, replacing the annual cliff-edge funding cycle
  • Replaces Household Support Fund: The CRF consolidates the Household Support Fund and Discretionary Housing Payments into a single streamlined grant

"This represents a genuine safety net to help families with the cost of living, giving councils the long-term certainty they need to plan ahead and deliver lasting change."

The two-child limit, introduced in 2017, has been widely criticized by child poverty campaigners for restricting benefits to larger families. Its removal is part of the government's landmark Child Poverty Strategy aimed at delivering "security, opportunity, and respect for families across the UK."

The simplified approach under the new fund is expected to reduce administrative burden on councils while ensuring families can access support when they need it.

Source: UK Government - Department for Work and Pensions (Published February 3, 2026)
