Fighting Disrupts Humanitarian Operations

Since yesterday, fighting in and around the town of Bule has claimed the lives of at least two civilians and injured several others, according to humanitarian partners on the ground. The violence has forced the suspension of food distributions that were scheduled to begin today.

More than 78,000 displaced people in and around Bule haven't been able to receive assistance since early December, leaving families facing critical shortages of food, healthcare, and clean water.

Ongoing Crisis in the Sahel Region

Ituri province continues to face one of the most severe humanitarian crises in the region, driven by conflict, large-scale displacement, climate shocks, food insecurity, malnutrition, and disease outbreaks.

OCHA stated it continues to be in close contact with local authorities, adapting operations as conditions change on the ground.

UN Calls for Protection of Civilians

The UN reminded all parties of their obligations under international humanitarian law to protect civilians and ensure the safety of humanitarian workers and assets.

"We need safe, predictable and unimpeded humanitarian access to prevent a further deterioration of the situation," OCHA stated.
